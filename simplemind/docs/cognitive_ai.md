# Cognitive AI

SimpleMind serves as a platform for building [Cognitive AI systems](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/8713837):
* "A cognitive system views the mind as a highly parallel information processor, uses various models for representing information, and employs algorithms for transforming and reasoning with the information."
* "A cognitive architecture is a blueprint for developing cognitive systems. It specifies predetermined structures and interactions among them with the goal of achieving functions of the mind. They constrain the types of cognitive models that can be developed. The knowledge embodied in the architecture drives the interactions among the structures to achieve intelligent behavior."

Cognitive AI emphasizes human-like intelligence:
* We are exploring what can be accomplished with human-like thinking (and discovering or gaining insights on how it might work).
* Humans create foundation models from infancy (largely unsupervised)
    - These models seem to provide a very strong general basis (foundation) for learning specific tasks (or being taught).
    - For foot contact detection: Jill does not need to train me on pose/joint detection, just teach me or tell me which is needed (examples are helpful)
* SM enables few shot ~~learning~~ teaching
    - Combining this with foundation models (possibly fine tuned) is very powerful
    - It is human-like &rArr; better able to cooperate, more transparency and trust, more human agency in AI development
    - May also be human level (capable of being autonomous even in high stakes situations)

SimpleMind enables:
* hybrid cognitive systems, combining neural networks with machine reasoning
    * increasing reliability and trust
    * rules can be used while accumulating data (prior to training), to handle specific situations, and to check results
* aggregation and tuning of processing tools into complex systems
    * [nnU-Net showed](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.09556) that "careful implementation and configuration of the architecture are more crucial for achieving state-of-the-art results than modifying the architecture itself"
* the manufacturing of clinically usable AI at scale
    * to address the [Health AI Paradox](https://www.spiedigitallibrary.org/conference-proceedings-of-spie/13411/3055542/Tackling-the-health-AI-paradox/10.1117/12.3055542.full): "researched models aren't implemented, implemented models aren't researched"
